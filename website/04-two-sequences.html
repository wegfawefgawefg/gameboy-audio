Chapter 4

Two pulse channels means two note sequences.

The Game Boy has two pulse voices. One can carry a melody while the other plays bass, harmony, or a second pattern.

Spelled Out

num_samples = int(1.6 * sample_rate)

samples = []
for _ in range(num_samples):
    samples.append(0)
# Start with silence: one zero offset for every sample slot.

melody = [
    (523.25, 0.00, 0.18),  # C5
    (493.88, 0.20, 0.18),  # B4
    (392.00, 0.40, 0.18),  # G4
    (329.63, 0.60, 0.28),  # E4
]

bass = [
    (261.63, 0.00, 0.38),  # C4
    (392.00, 0.40, 0.38),  # G4
]

for frequency, start, duration in melody:
    add_pulse_note(samples, frequency, start, duration, volume=0.18, duty=0.5)

for frequency, start, duration in bass:
    add_pulse_note(samples, frequency, start, duration, volume=0.16, duty=0.25)

Why This Matters

Melody + Bass

One pulse voice plays the tune. The other anchors the harmony.

Two Duties

A 50% duty and a 25% duty can sound different even at similar pitches.

Mixing

The final output is still one list of offsets. Each voice adds into it.
