Chapter 4

Two voices means two offset lists added together.

The Game Boy has two pulse channels. They can play the same kind of sound at different pitches, volumes, and duty cycles.

Spelled Out

sample_rate = 44100
seconds = 1
num_samples = seconds * sample_rate

mixed_samples = []

for sample_index in range(num_samples):
    time = sample_index / sample_rate

    phase_a = (220 * time) % 1
    phase_b = (330 * time) % 1

    # Voice A: 50% duty pulse.
    if phase_a < 0.5:
        voice_a = 1
    else:
        voice_a = -1

    # Voice B: 25% duty pulse.
    # The high part is shorter, so it sounds thinner.
    if phase_b < 0.25:
        voice_b = 1
    else:
        voice_b = -1

    # Mix by adding offsets.
    # Use smaller volumes so the sum does not get too large.
    mixed = voice_a * 0.22 + voice_b * 0.18
    mixed_samples.append(mixed)

What Can You Do With Two Pulse Voices?

Melody + Harmony

One channel plays the tune while the other plays a supporting note.

Arpeggios

Switch notes quickly and the ear hears a chord-like pattern.

Thicker Lead

Put both channels near each other in pitch to make a bigger sound.
